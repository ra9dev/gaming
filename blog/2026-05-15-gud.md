### Why does navigation feel fluid?
![ACHV_POTION_CRAFT](/img/gaming/ACHV_POTION_CRAFT.png)

After fully completing PotionCraft, I fell so in love with their limited set of locations and the store navigation that I decided to just take this approach and adapt it for my game about a voodoo doll shop.

In short, the world of PotionCraft consists of 2D locations of different rooms, with the camera looking straight down at a 90-degree angle.
**To move between rooms, the player uses the WASD keys and essentially "flies" from the bedroom to the laboratory, then to the basement or behind the sales counter.**
There’s no default walking character like in most game engine templates — only the camera.

And these are exactly the kind of design limitations that bring ergonomics to the game, significantly improving the player’s quality of life.
The familiar movement keybinds from larger AAA games work predictably, yet are much simpler at the same time.
